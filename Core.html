(1.0) BIM Execution Plan

Back to Index...

Objective - Development of BIM Execution Plan document that could be utilized as a project guideline throughout the project cycle.

(1.1)

Introduction

BIM Execution Plan is a constitutional rule-book for the project covering all topics required for execution of the project. No collaborative BIM activities involving more than one stakeholder could be performed without agreement on BEP document. The BEP author should discuss terms of services with relevant stakeholders prior to the development of the document. All information provided in the document to be countersigned by relevant stakeholders.Any query arising between project stakeholders regarding methodology, content, scope or any such information required for the BIM execution, should be resolved as per mentioned in BEP only. In case the resolution agreement differs than mentioned in BEP, a BEP amendment along with revised modifications to be issued by ‘BIM Manager’ to the project stakeholders.

(2.5)

Tender Stage Drawings Standards

The Drawings to be considered as a Tender Stage Delivery shall follow following standards along with the same mentioned in BIM Execution Plan:

  1. Tender Stage drawings shall have all information required to float the tender of an item highlighted/tagged in the drawings.
  2. The tagging provided in the drawings shall match with the tagging provided to the same item in BOQ.
  3. All drawings shall have legend explaning tags and modifications in visibility graphics for perticular item.
  4. Visibility Graphics, Tags annotation strategy shall be at descretion of individual modeling stakeholder.
  5. The naming convention as per mentioned in the Chapter 14 of BSCI:2021-2022 shall be followed for all kind of drawings to be considered as delivery.
  6. All tags for the BIM elements on the drawings must be automatically generated via BIM software.
  7. Items mentioned in BIM Exclusions in Chapter 16 of BCSI:2021-2022 are allowed to be shown in 2D manually on Tender Stage drawings.
  8. All drawing to be considered as a project delivery shall be exported from the models only. Any other source of the drawings shall not be considered in Tender Stage delivery submission.
  9. Any kind of modifications in predefined List of Drawings mentioned in BIM Execultion Plan to be updated on regular basis. Concerned stakeholders shall notify BIM Manager in case of such modifications, BIM Manager shall update the same in BIM Execution Plan and issue an amendment.

(2.6)

Tender Stage Modeling Standards

The Models to be considered as a Tender Stage Delivery shall be compliant with following standards along with the same mentioned in BIM Execution Plan and Chapter 15 of BCSI:2021-2022:

  1. The Tagging, Parameters and any other information incorporation into the models shall be as per BIM Execution Plan.
  2. The item inclusion/exclusions and level of development shall be as per BIM Scope.
  3. There should be consistancy in tagging applied to items in BIM models and tagging adopted for the same in BOQ.
  4. The methodology to adopt tagging methodology and information incorporation into the models shall be at discretion of individual stakeholders.
  5. A Model shall follow model breakdown structure methodolgy adopted for the project.
  6. The model shall be developed clash free with tolerance mentioned in BIM Execution Plan.

(2.7)

Tender Stage Quantitiy Extraction Standards

The tender stage quantities extracted from the models shall be as per following standards:

  1. The parameters required to be extracted along with the quantities shall be pre agreed and defined by BIM Authors and BIM Manager prior commencement of any extraction work.
  2. The minimum parameters for an individual items should be as follows:
    1. Item Tag
    2. Identity
    3. Size
    4. Level
    5. Space
    6. Method
    7. Quantity
    8. Unit

    Example:
    Parameter Item Tags Identity Level Size Space Method Quantity Unit
    Example RC01 Gypsum Grid Ceiling Ground Floor 600 x 600 mm Workstation Area Area 800.00 SQM
    RC01 Gypsum Grid Ceiling Second Floor 600 x 600 mm Cafeteria Area Area 467.21 SQM
    D03 Single Wooden Door Second Floor 2100 x 900 mm Manager's Cabin Count 3 NOS
  3. The Item tags should match with BOQ tags for the same line item.
  4. All quantities extracted from the BIM Model must be directly and automatically exported from the respective BIM models only. Any kind of manual quantity input shall be exlpicitly mentioned in BIM Execution Plan prior commencement of work and should be discouraged wherever possible.
  5. Value for the parameters not applicable for an individual item shall be kept as 'NULL'. (example - Space parameter shall not be applicable for a Wall Item since the item may attached to two spaces

(15.0) General Modeling Standards

Back to Index...

(15.1)

Level/Floor

A level/floor is defined as the area between Top of Slab (TOS) of a level/floor and Bottom of Slab or Structural Framing element of level/floor exactly above. However, the slab including the structural elements of the level/floor from where TOS is calculated shall be included in current level. Level/Floor name should contain the ‘spot elevation’ followed by ‘name of the trade’ where particular models have been authored followed by ‘Level Information’. E.g. For Level 2 in the Architectural Model the level annotation should be annotated as +6000MM AR LEVEL 2.

(15.2)

Units

Metric Units shall be used restricted till 2 decimal places. Areas to be calculated in Square Meters.

(15.3)

Base Point/Spot Level Calculations

There shall be two spot level dimensioned to be calculated and adopted i.e. Distance from Ground Level and Real World Coordinates from Sea level

(15.4)

Survey Point

Project Survey point to be established at the intersection of the first grids. The Survey point should be considered as Reference Point (0,0,0) for the particular project and all stakeholders to implement the same into respective BIM models. Survey Point should also contain Real World Coordinates.

(15.5)

Annotation Style

All kinds of annotations shall be kept in CAPITAL for the entire project. Font and Style could be changed as per individual stakeholder requirement

(15.6)

Scale

Only following scales to be used for the development of drawings purpose:

  1. 1:5
  2. 1:10
  3. 1:20
  4. 1:50
  5. 1:100
  6. 1:200
  7. 1:500
  8. 1:1000
  9. 1:2000
  10. 1:5000

(15.7)

2D Export

Item wise and project stage wise (i.e. Government Sanctioning, Fire NOC) level,style,color,transparency, lineweight assignment to be done prior extraction of the drawings from the models. If BIM software allows then projects stagewise templates to be developed by BIM Manager and Team prior commencement of any kind of work in the models. Drawings to be extracted in following formats only:

  1. dwg
  2. pdf

(15.8)

3D Export

.ifc 2x3 or .nwd shall be adopted as 3D file export format by the stakeholders that are not responsible for Model authoring. All model authoring teams shall be using the same Authoring Tool only.

(15.9)

Authoring

Only BIM modeling team shall be responsible for all kinds of geometry incorporation into the BIM models.

(15.10)

BIM Exclusions

Following items shall be excluded for BIM modeling purpose:

  1. Items less than or equal to 25mm in any kind of size dimension.
  2. Items that could not be developed in 3D.
  3. Surface Finishes such as paint.
  4. Items that is not in current project’s BIM Scope
  5. Flexible Wiring
  6. Items that could be derived from other less detailed 3D items. (Example - Hinges)

(15.11)

Object Hosting

Objects hosted on perticular level should not be extended to another level. In case, object size allows object to be visible on another level, the object should be spilt at the level junctions.

(15.12)

Compliance

Item naming, BIM file naming, and all other items shall be as per mentioned in BCSI:2021-2022 and BIM Execution Plan.

(15.13)

Material Tags

All geometrical items shall be comprised of materials along with pre classified tags.

(15.14)

Model Size Limitations

BIM Models extending size of 100mb shall be divided and seperate models to be maintained for individual division.

(15.15)

Hygiene

All kinds of unwanted BIM model links, 2D drawings links, data links, imported Models, imports 2D data, Imags shall be removed prior inter-stakeholder transaction of the BIM model. Any kind of unwanetd item shall be highlighted by reviewer during coordination meetings.

(15.16)

Modeling Scope

Stakeholders not responsible for modeling a trade shall not be encouraged to model elements from such trade. (Example - Stakeholders responsible for development of Plumbing Trade model shall not be encouraged to develop a structural wall in any kind of BIM Project Model.

(15.17)

Common Precautions

Following precautions must be taken by BIM Author:

  1. Hosting Level of an Item should be checked prior modeling.
  2. All gaps that might result in quantity variation/discrepency shall be eliminated.
  3. Individual Item paramters, tags, size should be checked prior modeling.
  4. A manual intereferece check should be performed on regular frequecy during Modeling.
  5. List of Drawings shall also be kept within the models if drawings extraction is in scope.
  6. Regular Backups must be maintaned.

Methodology

  1. All BIM geometrical items to be classified on the basis of discipline (e.g. Architetcure, Structure, etc)

  3. There shall be two kinds of tests, Self-descipline and inter-descipline.
  4. Self Discipline clash tests to be prioritised on the basis of potential modification impact and rigidity of individual item.   5. Inter Discipline clash tests to be prioritised on the basis of construction sequencing and potential impact of modification of individual item.
  6. Same preference/prioritisation methodology to be followed while inter-trade coordination related discussions during coordination meeting.
  7. Tolerance to be adopted as per mentioned in BIM Execution Plan

(19.1)

Architecture

  1. All BIM activities should be commenced after finalisation or approval on designs from the client.
  2. Special Items such elevational feature, specially designed compound walls, parametric elements to be developed prior commencement of BIM Modeling work. A spearate reusable item repository should be maintained by stakeholders resopnsible for Architecture BIM Modeling.
  3. Floor Plans for approvals (RERA, DCR, Completion Certificates, NBFC Certificate, Occupancy Certificates), marketing broucher and for any other use shall derived from BIM Model.
  4. There shall not be any simultanious development of drawings actvities to be undertaken unless notified to client and BIM Manager.
  5. Design exploration, energy simulation related activites to be performed on separate models.
  6. Separate copy BIM models to be utilised for visualisation purpose.
  7. Material Library must be maintained with item coding and same to be imported into BIM models and utlised for further BIM Activities.
  8. Graphics standards for individual items such as linewight, scale, type are at decretion of individual stakeholder. But shall be mentioned in BIM Execution Plan.
  9. Paint quantities to be calculated manually on the basis of 2D drawings.
  10. Items where quantities to be calcualted in numbers shall be modeled as generic representing outer boundries and information required for BIM Utilizers Team and coordination.

(19.2)

Structure

  1. Structural Analysis related activites should be completed prior commencement of BIM Modeling activties.
  2. Reinforcement incorporation into BIM models shall be as per scope of work. There should be seperate Structural BIM Model to be developed for the purpose.
  3. Reinforcement and similar detail work could be allowed to developed in 2D.
  4. It shall be assssumed that there shall be minor quantification discrepancy at the structural elements junctions due to software shortcomings.
  5. Quantities for excavation should not be recommended to be extracted from the BIM model to be used for throughout the project.
  6. Site Marking related activites shall be done simulatanious to BIM activities and not required to be shown in BIM models.
  7. Tensile Structures Fabric item shall be utilised only for the coordination purpose only.
  8. Cutting and Filling activities shall not be covered in BIM.
  9. Scaffolding and fabrication similar temporary items shall be as per individual project scope of work.
  10. Sleeves more than 50 mm dia shall be incorporated into the models.

(19.3)

Interior

  1. All project specific materials, BIM Item models, cost coding, packagewise tags should be developed prior commencement of any BIM Modeling Activity.
  2. The design should be finalised from the client prior commencement of any modeling work.
  3. A proper study to be undertaken to realise the effort required for an item to be incorporated in BIM and same if done by traditional manual method. On the basis of study final BIM Scope to be decided.
  4. It shall be duty of BIM Manager to realise the client about software limitation prior finalisation of scope of work.
  5. Detail drawings shall be allowed to be developed in 2D manually.
  6. Vendor Package could be incorporated into the BIM Models after Tender Stage. However, changes to be avoided wherever possible.

(19.4)

Landscape/Infra-structure

  1. Plantation and softscapes shall be excluded from the scope.
  2. Detailed drawings shall be developed in 2D manually.
  3. Grading and earthworks related items could be excluded from BIM Scope.
  4. Any kind of site counter must be incorporated into the models. Same to be matching with survey levels.

(19.5)

Mechanical

  1. Flexible Ducts and Hangers supports exclusion / inclusion shall be disccused prior commencement of any modeling work.
  2. Fabrication BIM Models to be provided by vendors. In case unavailibility from vendors, exclusion/exclusion of such items shall be dicussed and finalised with BIM Manager prior commencement of Modeling Work.
  3. Sleeves requirements to be conveyed to Stuctural Modeling team in initial stages of the project.

(19.6)

Electrical

  1. Conduits, wiring shall be excluded from BIM Modeling Scope.
  2. Wiring is not required to be shown in the cable trays.
  3. Sleeves requirements to be conveyed to Stuctural Modeling team in initial stages of the project.

(19.7)

Plumbing and Fire Fighting

  1. Flexible Pipes shall be modelled approximately.
  2. Pipes within Walls shall be ignored during model auditing.
  3. Sleeves requirements to be conveyed to Stuctural Modeling team in initial stages of the project.
  4. Fire Spriklers and Final Fittings to be modeled as generic models representing outer boundries and information required for BIM Utilizers Team and coordination.
  5. Equipments to be modeled as generic representing outer boundries and information required for BIM Utilizers Team.
